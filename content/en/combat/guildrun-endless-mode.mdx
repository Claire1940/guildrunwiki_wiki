<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding Guildrun Endless Mode and Difficulty Scaling">

**Guildrun endless mode** represents the pinnacle of the roguelike autobattler experience developed by Leyline. While the standard game features eight escalating difficulty levels, Endless Mode removes the ceiling, allowing players to test their builds against infinite enemy growth. Success in this mode is not just about having high-level heroes; it is about understanding how the game's 75 specialization paths and 300+ relics interact when combat lasts significantly longer than standard encounters.

In this environment, traditional "burst" strategies often fall off. Instead, players must focus on **multi-class combinations** and **shared-effect builds** that gain value over time. The leaderboard scoring system rewards not just the distance reached, but the efficiency of the run, making every Shard spent in the shop a critical decision for your long-term survival.

| Difficulty | Unlock Requirement | Primary Challenge | Preparation Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Difficulty 1-4** | Initial Progression | Basic loop mastery | Build balanced teams with 1 tank/1 DPS |
| **Difficulty 5-7** | Clear previous level | Reduced shop efficiency | Focus on specific specialization synergy |
| **Difficulty 8** | Clear Difficulty 7 | Optimized boss pressure | Maximize rank upgrades and relic value |
| **Endless Mode** | Complete standard run | Infinite enemy scaling | Prioritize scalable sustain and % damage |

<Callout type="warning" title="Scaling Warning">
Enemy health and damage in Endless Mode scale exponentially. After the first few rifts, non-scalable flat damage items become obsolete. Always look for relics that provide percentage-based buffs or effects triggered by attack frequency.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Preparation for Endless Progression">

Entering **Guildrun endless mode** without a plan is a recipe for a quick exit. Players should treat the initial rifts as a "setup phase" where the goal is to build an economic and equipment foundation that can support the team once the difficulty spikes. Follow these steps to maximize your run potential.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Economic Foundation">
    In the first 5 rifts, prioritize **Early Economy Relics**. These allow for additional shop rerolls and cheaper recruitment, which are essential for finding the exact 180 specializations needed for your final build.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Role Stabilization">
    Establish a "Stable Three" core: one primary tank, one primary carry, and one support. Do not spread your rank upgrades too thin; a single S-Rank hero is often more valuable than three C-Rank heroes in early Endless stages.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Specialization Pivot">
    Around the mid-game, evaluate your shop offers. If the game provides high-tier **Ranged** or **Duelist** items, use the **Respec** mechanic to align your heroes' specialization paths with your best equipment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Endless Optimization">
    Once your core build is locked, focus entirely on **Scalable Class Bonuses**. Look for relics that reward repeated attacks or long battle duration, as fights will naturally take longer as you progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Reserve Units">
Don't ignore your reserve slots. In Endless Mode, having a specialized "Boss Killer" or "Crowd Control" hero in reserve can save a run when you encounter a specific enemy type that counters your main active formation.
</Callout>

</Section>
