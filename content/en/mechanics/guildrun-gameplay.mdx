<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding Core Gameplay Mechanics">

Guildrun is a deep auto-battler rogue-lite that challenges players to manage hero units, class synergies, and relic effects simultaneously. Success in a run depends on your ability to pivot your team composition based on the shop's offerings and the specific threats encountered in each combat encounter.

The flow of combat involves placing your heroes strategically to trigger specific class bonuses. Vanguards are essential for survivability, while Mages and Assassins provide the necessary burst to clear waves. Understanding the "Rush" mechanic—where abilities refresh or gain power under specific conditions—is the key to scaling your damage output into the later stages of a run.

| Class | Primary Role | Key Stat Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Vanguard | Tanking, CC | Max HP, Armor |
| Mage | Magic Damage | Magic, Mana Regen |
| Duelist | Attack Speed | Attack Speed, Crit |
| Assassin | Burst Damage | Crit, Attack |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategic Positioning">
Always place your Vanguards in the front row to ensure they absorb initial enemy aggression. This protects your fragile Mages and Assassins, allowing them to stack damage-over-time or crit buffs safely.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Hero Synergy and Builds">

Building a cohesive team in Guildrun requires selecting units that share class tags or benefit from the same relic types. You should aim to specialize in one or two damage profiles, such as "Crit-Frost" or "Rush-Stall," rather than spreading your stats too thin across multiple conflicting archetypes.

<Callout type="warning" title="Synergy Caution">
Avoid over-investing in too many classes. Aim for 2-3 major synergies to maximize your relic efficiency. A team with too many disparate classes will struggle to gain meaningful bonuses from relics.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Progression Steps">

Efficient progression through a run requires careful management of your shards. Use the following steps to optimize your shop phases and combat preparation.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Team Setup">
    Draft a balanced team during the early stages. Prioritize at least one Vanguard for tanking and one damage dealer to ensure you survive the first three combats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Relic Prioritization">
    Select relics that complement your current hero classes. If you have multiple Warriors, prioritize relics that boost "Crit" or "Rush" abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Economic Management">
    Use rerolls sparingly. Aim to keep a buffer of shards to maximize interest or to snag high-tier units when they appear in the shop.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Endgame Optimization">
    Sell low-impact units to upgrade your core carries to S-rank. Ensure your best items are equipped on the heroes that benefit most from their specific stat bonuses.
  </Step>
</Steps>
