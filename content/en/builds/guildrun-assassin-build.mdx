For the assassin archetype, you want to focus heavily on items that grant flat critical strike chance, physical and magical omnivamp, and poison application. <Section icon={Shield} title="Rush vs. Stall Mechanics in Assassin Builds">

Guildrun features two opposing combat tempos: **Rush** and **Stall**. Understanding how to balance these two mechanics is crucial for tailoring your assassin build to different enemy compositions.

| Combat Phase | Activation Condition | Primary Stat Bonuses | Ideal Build Application |
| Rush | First 6 to 10 seconds of combat | Increased attack speed, movement speed, and attack power | Fast burst setups designed to wipe out backlines instantly |
| Stall | After 15 to 20 seconds of combat | High defense, percentage stat scaling, and shields | Defensive setups or poison builds that rely on survival |

For an assassin build, **Rush** is generally the superior choice. Because your units start in stealth with guaranteed critical strikes, you want to deal as much damage as possible within the first few seconds of the fight. Items that extend the duration of Rush abilities allow your assassins to clean up the battlefield before the enemy can retaliate.

However, if you are facing highly defensive tanks or bosses with massive health pools, integrating some **Stall** mechanics can save your run. Relics that grant team-wide shields or massive defensive boosts after 20 seconds ensure that your poison stacks have enough time to tick down and secure the kill.

<Callout type="warning" title="Stun Danger">
Beware of enemies with heavy area-of-effect stuns. If your assassins are stunned during the initial Rush phase, they will lose their stealth window and can be easily burst down by enemy backline damage.
</Callout>

</Section>
