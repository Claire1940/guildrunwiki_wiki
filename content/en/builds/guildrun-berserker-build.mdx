<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Mechanics of the Guildrun Berserker Build">

The cornerstone of the **guildrun berserker build** revolves around maximizing high-risk, high-reward melee scaling. In Guildrun, the premier unit for this playstyle is Rip, a high-tempo Warrior who excels at turning incoming damage into raw offensive power. Unlike traditional tanks that rely solely on damage mitigation, a Berserker thrives on maintaining a low health pool to trigger devastating stat multipliers.

Rip utilizes a unique Rage mechanic that activates when he enters combat. Upon casting his active ability, he enters a state of frenzy, gaining massive bonuses to his maximum health, attack speed, and Omnivamp. This makes him incredibly difficult to bring down once he starts attacking, as his life steal scales directly with his damage output. Furthermore, whenever Rip or an adjacent ally drops below the 50% health threshold, all of his Rush abilities instantly refresh, and he gains a flat 25% attack speed boost. This reset mechanic allows him to chain high-impact abilities back-to-back, shredding through enemy frontlines before they can react.

To make this build work effectively, you must understand the "Rush" system. Rush abilities are high-impact buffs that trigger under specific conditions, such as the start of combat or during active skill casts. By stacking items that extend the duration of these Rush states, you can keep Rip at peak combat efficiency for the entirety of the round, bypassing the standard downtime that plagues other melee builds.

<Callout type="success" title="Synergy Tip">
Always pair Rip with heroes who benefit from rapid health fluctuations. Because Rip's passive triggers when adjacent allies drop below 50% HP, placing a secondary off-tank next to him can force early Rush resets without putting Rip in immediate lethal danger.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Class Synergies & Combat Variants">

Unlocking the full potential of this build requires a deep understanding of Guildrun's class synergy system. By combining the Warrior class with complementary roles like Duelist, Vanguard, and Mystic, you can build a highly resilient squad capable of tackling high-difficulty encounters and deep Endless Mode runs.

When Rip gains the Warrior class synergy, his base offensive capabilities skyrocket. Warriors gain a flat bonus of 50 attack, and their Permafrost damage is enabled to land critical strikes. This opens up a highly effective frost-crit hybrid build. If you pair Rip with a frost-applying mage like Sal, every critical hit Rip lands will inflict frost, which in turn reduces the enemy's armor and attack speed. This creates a powerful crowd-control loop that keeps enemies permanently slowed while maximizing Rip's physical damage output.

| Class | Role | Synergy Rating | Key Benefit |

| Assassin | Executor | ★★★★☆ | Instantly kills targets below **25% HP** |

For players looking to maximize raw damage, the critical strike conversion mechanic is essential. In Guildrun, critical strike rate is not capped at 100%. Any excess crit rate you accumulate through relics and items is automatically converted into bonus critical damage. When combined with Rip's rapid attack speed, this allows his basic attacks to hit for astronomical numbers during his Rage window.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Optimal Gear & Relic Setup">

Itemization can make or break your run. Because the Berserker relies on maintaining a balance between taking damage and healing back up, your item choices must support both survivability and high-frequency skill casting. Stacking pure defensive items like armor will reduce the damage Rip takes, which can actually slow down his Rage transition. Instead, focus on health pools, shields, and attack speed.

The most critical offensive relic for this build is the Crown of Haste, which grants a massive 40% attack speed boost during the first 8 seconds of combat. This ensures Rip can build up his Rage stacks immediately at the start of the round. For defensive utility, look for Vindictive Shields. This relic grants a lasting shield at the start of combat and deals damage to adjacent enemies equal to a percentage of the remaining shield value, turning defensive mitigation into passive offensive output.

| Relic Name | Rarity | Tier | Primary Effect |
| Crown of Haste | Epic | S-Tier | Increases **attack speed** by 40% during Rush |
| Vindictive Shields | Rare | A-Tier | Deals damage based on **remaining shields** |
| Mystery Charm | Rare | B-Tier | Grants a random **rare offensive relic** |
| Shard Magnet | Common | C-Tier | Increases **shard generation** on combat victory |

When managing your inventory, always compare how items scale with Rip's active abilities. Items that offer flat health boosts are highly valuable because Rip's Rage ability scales his maximum health by a percentage. A larger base health pool results in significantly larger scaling bonuses, which in turn boosts his passive health-regen mechanics.

| Item Name | Type | Scaling Type | Best Used For |
| Vanguard Emblem | Defensive | Max HP % | Boosting **shield values** on Rip |
| Eye of Haste | Offensive | Attack Speed | Extending **Rush duration** by 3 seconds |
| Permafrost Shard | Offensive | Crit Rate | Enabling **frost damage** to land critical hits |
| Salvation Scroll | Defensive | Health Regen | Reviving **fallen allies** after 20 seconds |

<Callout type="warning" title="Gear Caution">
Avoid equipping items that grant flat armor without health scaling. High armor values prevent Rip's health from dropping to the 50% threshold quickly, which can delay his crucial Rush resets and leave your backline vulnerable.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Combat Positioning & Team Composition">

Positioning is a critical aspect of Guildrun's tactical gameplay. Because Rip is a melee unit, placing him incorrectly can result in him being focused down by ranged enemies or trapped by high-defense enemy tanks before he can trigger his Rage state.

Your ideal team composition should feature a solid mix of frontline defense, rapid physical damage, and utility crowd control. Rowan should always occupy the primary frontline tile to pull the initial wave of attacks. Place Rip directly adjacent to Rowan, slightly offset to the left or right. This positioning ensures that when Rowan's health drops below 50%, Rip's Rush abilities refresh instantly, while keeping Rip safe from direct single-target burst attacks.

| Hero Name | Rank | Synergy Type | Combat Priority |
| Rip | S-Rank | Rush Carry | Target **highest HP** enemy and trigger rage |
| Rowan | A-Rank | Stall Tank | Hold the **frontline** and heal based on armor |
| Nyx | S-Rank | Combo DPS | Clean up **low health** targets with multi-hits |
| Sal | B-Rank | Crit Mage | Apply **Permafrost** and burn debuffs to targets |

When facing enemies with piercing line attacks or wide AoE spells, avoid grouping your units in a straight vertical or horizontal line. Instead, utilize a split formation. Place your primary tank on one side of the board to draw the linear attacks, while Rip and your backup damage dealers like Nyx flank from the opposite side to dismantle the enemy's backline.

<Callout type="success" title="Positioning Success">
Using a diagonal layout prevents enemy mages from landing high-damage AoE spells on both Rowan and Rip simultaneously. This keeps your Berserker's health pool stable enough to sustain through life steal.
</Callout>
