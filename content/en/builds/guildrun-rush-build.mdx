The **guildrun rush build** is currently one of the most powerful strategies for tackling S-difficulty and Endless modes. Unlike standard builds that rely heavily on static item stats, the Rush engine focuses on dynamic scaling. Every time a hero triggers a Rush ability, the entire team (specifically Warriors) gains permanent offensive and defensive buffs that last for the duration of the run. This allows your squad to outscale enemies even when they become "deadlier" in later stages.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Progression Guide">

Building a successful **guildrun rush build** requires a disciplined approach to the shop and combat encounters. You cannot simply pick high-rarity items; you must pick items that facilitate more triggers or provide utility that Warriors lack, such as magic resist or omnivamp.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Early Game Setup">
    Focus on recruiting at least two Warriors with the Rush tag. Do not worry about item rarity yet; prioritize survival and triggering the Rush mechanic as often as possible to start the scaling process.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Mid-Game Optimization">
    Look for the Warrior Banner in the Guild shop. This is the most important relic for the build. Start focusing on Nicl's "Item Consumption" quest to clear up inventory space while retaining power.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Late Game Scaling">
    Transition into debuff items. Items that inflict Burn or Poison on hit are essential because they scale with the massive attack stats you have accumulated. Aim for 100+ Rush triggers before the final boss.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Endless Mode Transition">
    In Endless mode, focus entirely on "Infinite Scaling" relics. Choose rewards that increase your primary stats per shard or per combat victory. At this stage, your shields should be in the tens of thousands.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Stage | Priority Focus | Key Milestone |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Early** | Recruitment | 2+ Warriors in squad |
| **Mid** | Relics | Warrior Banner acquired |
| **Late** | Scaling | 1,000+ Attack on main DPS |
| **Endless** | Survival | Floor 15+ cleared |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Warning">
In S-difficulty, remember that heroes can only equip one item per rank. Do not waste your limited slots on flat health items; prioritize items with "per trigger" or "per crit" effects.
</Callout>

</Section>

The effectiveness of a **guildrun rush build** is heavily influenced by your choice of relics. While the heroes provide the base, the relics provide the multipliers. Specifically, you want to look for anything that interacts with "Warrior," "Crit," or "Rush."

- **S-Tier**: Warrior Banner, Lightning Mirror, Poison Catalyst.

Once your **guildrun rush build** is established, positioning and debuff management become the primary focus. You want your Warriors to be adjacent to as many enemies as possible to maximize AOE Rush triggers, but you must also protect your "Trigger Generators" from being burst down by elite units.

In Endless mode, enemies will eventually gain "Deadly" modifiers. To counter this, you must rely on "Lasting Shields." Unlike standard shields that decay over time, Lasting Shields persist until broken. This is crucial for surviving the initial 10 seconds of a high-floor Endless fight where enemy damage is at its peak.

| Debuff Type | Rush Build Synergy | Scaling Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Burn** | Scales with Max HP / +1 Magic per stack | High |
| **Poison** | Deals % damage over time / Increases with Rush | Extreme |
| **Frost** | Slows attack speed / Reduces enemy triggers | Medium |
| **Stun** | Triggered on Warrior Crits | High |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy Tip">
If you encounter a choice between flat attack and omnivamp in the late game, always choose omnivamp. With 2,000+ attack, a small percentage of lifesteal will keep your heroes at full health regardless of enemy damage.
</Callout>
