<Section icon={Swords} title="Hero Recruitment & Team Build Strategy">

Building an effective guild requires a deep understanding of unit roles and how they interact on the battlefield. The game moves away from simple damage-per-second metrics, forcing players to balance defensive block values, healing output, crowd control, and clean execution mechanics. 

When drafting your team, you should generally structure your active roster around three primary combat roles to ensure you can handle diverse enemy encounters.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Frontline Protectors">
    - **Primary Job**: Absorb initial enemy strikes and generate block values.
    - **Key Stats**: Maximum health, armor rating, and crowd-control duration.
    - **Top Specialization**: Defensive tanking and protective shields.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Backline Carries">
    - **Primary Job**: Deliver sustained physical or magical damage from safety.
    - **Key Stats**: Attack speed, critical strike chance, and flat damage.
    - **Top Specialization**: Ranged marksman or high-burst assassin.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Utility & Supports">
    - **Primary Job**: Restore health, cleanse debuffs, and amplify ally damage.
    - **Key Stats**: Mana generation, healing power, and buff efficiency.
    - **Top Specialization**: Dedicated healer or hybrid offensive support.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

To maximize the efficiency of these roles, players must pay close attention to individual class specializations. The table below outlines how basic hero archetypes transition into specialized battlefield assets.

| Archetype | Primary Battlefield Job | Specialization Focus | Upgrade Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Tank | Absorb frontline pressure, protect allies | Durability, control, defense | Defensive rank upgrades |
| Healer | Restore health, stabilize party sustain | Healing frequency, protection | Reliable healing output |
| Ranged | Deal consistent backline damage | Attack speed, sustained scaling | Raw damage consistency |
| Assassin | Eliminate high-priority targets quickly | Burst damage, offensive scaling | Reliable finishing power |

<Callout type="warning" title="Drafting Trap">
Do not fill your bench entirely with fragile damage dealers. A single high-damage carry supported by two durable tanks and a dedicated healer will consistently outperform a team of four unshielded damage dealers.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide for Your First Successful Run">

Completing your first full run requires careful planning from the very first shop screen. Follow this step-by-step progression plan to stabilize your economy and secure your first victory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish a Stable Frontline First">
    Prioritize recruiting a durable frontline unit in the opening round. If the shop offers protective shields or defensive items, secure them immediately to minimize early health loss.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify Your Primary Carry">
    By round three, select a dedicated damage dealer to build your team around. Look for ranged units or duelists that scale well with attack-speed items or physical damage bonuses.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Synergize With Shared Class Effects">
    As your roster expands, look for heroes that share class traits or specific mechanical keywords. Activating shared bonuses significantly boosts your team's baseline power without costing extra shards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Transition to Advanced Specializations">
    Once your core units reach higher ranks, commit to specialization paths that cover your team's current weaknesses. Avoid hybrid paths unless your frontline is already completely stable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

As you master this basic sequence, you can begin climbing the game's progressive difficulty ladder. Each tier introduces harsher modifiers that test the limits of your build.

| Tier | Unlock Requirement | Core Challenge Modifier | Recommended Preparation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Difficulty 1 | Available at start | Base progression, standard bosses | Balanced party, basic synergy |
| Difficulty 3 | Complete Difficulty 2 | Punishes weak frontlines, higher damage | Add defensive support, scale carry |
| Difficulty 5 | Complete Difficulty 4 | Inefficient shops punished, lower margins | Plan specialization paths early |
| Difficulty 8 | Complete Difficulty 7 | Hardest standard mode, aggressive scaling | Maximize econ, optimize relics |
| Endless Mode | Complete standard runs | Infinite scaling, leaderboard tracking | Scalable relics, repeated attacks |
