<Section icon={Target} title="Hero Specialization and Class Progression Guide">

With 25 heroes, 75 specialization paths, and 180 individual specializations planned for the full game, understanding the upgrade system is vital. Rank upgrades allow you to transform basic recruits into highly specialized combatants. Choosing complementary paths prevents your team from lacking essential defensive or offensive capabilities.

The progression system rewards players who plan their class transitions early. By matching your hero specializations with your active relics, you can create powerful combat loops that make even low-tier heroes perform exceptionally well.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Core Core">
    Recruit a balanced starting team of three heroes covering tank, damage, and support roles before spending resources on shop rerolls.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Specialized Paths">
    Choose rank upgrades that reinforce each hero's primary job, such as block for tanks or critical chance for carries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Incorporate Multiclass Synergies">
    Combine secondary specialization paths to create hybrid units, like a paladin that heals while absorbing frontline damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Strategic Respecs">
    Reset and reallocate your specialization choices when transitioning from early-game mob clearing to single-target boss fights.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Managing your rank upgrades efficiently is the difference between clearing a rift and failing during the mid-game. Avoid upgrading temporary units that you plan to swap out later, as this wastes valuable shards that could be spent on your permanent carries.

| Class Role | Primary Focus | Multiclass Value | Upgrade Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tank** | Durability, damage reduction, control | Combines well with self-healing paths | Defensive rank upgrades first |
| **Healer** | Restoration, protection, team sustain | Pairs with offensive support buffs | Healing frequency and output |
| **Ranged Carry** | Attack speed, sustained physical damage | Works with multi-target attack bonuses | Raw damage and crit chance |
| **Assassin** | Burst damage, single-target finishing | Pairs with high mobility and attack speed | Direct burst and execution damage |
| **Hybrid** | Adaptable support, defense, or damage | Fills any missing role in the party | Solving current team weaknesses |

<Callout type="warning" title="Respec Costs">
While respecing your heroes is highly effective for late-game boss fights, doing it too often can deplete your shard economy, leaving you with fewer resources for shop upgrades.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Equipment and Relic Optimization Checklist">

Items and relics shape the direction of your run. Items are equipped directly to individual heroes to boost their stats, while relics provide powerful, passive, team-wide effects. Prioritizing universal survival and economy-boosting relics early in a run maximizes your chances of surviving higher difficulty levels.

The community continuously updates the equipment tier list based on patch changes. Focus on items that scale with your hero's attack speed or defense, rather than flat single-stat boosts that lose value in late-game encounters.

| Tier | Relic Category | Best Compatible Classes | Key Power Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S-Tier** | Class-Scaling Relics | Shared-class team setups | Boosts multiple units simultaneously |
| **S-Tier** | Attack-Speed Effects | Ranged carries, duelists | Accelerates both damage and on-hit triggers |
| **S-Tier** | Early Economy Relics | Any starting composition | Compounds resources for late-game upgrades |
| **A-Tier** | Healing Amplification | Dedicated healers, paladins | Increases sustain during prolonged boss fights |
| **A-Tier** | Dedicated Damage | Assassins, single-target carries | Maximizes burst but lacks general flexibility |
| **B-Tier** | Conditional Combat | Specific synergy-focused builds | Extremely strong but requires exact setups |
| **C-Tier** | Single-Stat Items | Temporary early-game slots | Easily replaced by higher-tier equipment |
