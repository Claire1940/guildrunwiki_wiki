<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding the Guildrun Relic System">

In the complex ecosystem of Guildrun, relics represent the primary method of scaling your guild's power beyond simple hero levels. With more than 300 relics and 100 items available in the 2026 meta, distinguishing between a "bait" item and a run-defining artifact is the difference between a failed rift and a successful Difficulty 8 clearance. <Section icon={Target} title="A & B Tier: Situational and Role-Specific Power">

A-Tier and B-Tier relics are powerful but require specific conditions to shine. If you are running a "Lovers" shared-effect build or a single-carry scaling composition, these relics can often outperform S-tier options. However, they lack the flexibility to be picked in every run.

**A-Tier: Consistent Specialists**
These relics focus on amplifying specific roles like Healing or Dedicated Damage. They are excellent when you have already committed to a specialization path, such as a dedicated Healer or a burst-damage Assassin.

**B-Tier: The Conditional Gambles**
B-Tier relics include role-specific defensive items or relics that only trigger under strict conditions (e.g., "when a hero is below 20% health"). While they can save a run, they are often "dead weight" if your team is performing well.

| Tier | Category | Best Use Case | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A** | Healing Amplification | Defensive parties, Boss encounters | **Low** |
| **A** | Dedicated Damage | Single-carry backline builds | **Low** |
| **B** | Role Defense | Tanks exposed to heavy pressure | **Medium** |
| **B** | Conditional Combat | High-risk, high-reward burst builds | **High** |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid C-Tier Bait">
Low-impact single-stat items (C-Tier) provide small flat bonuses like +5 Health. These are rarely worth the Shard cost in the mid-game as they do not scale with your hero's growth or specializations.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="How to Pair Relics with Hero Specializations">

With 75 specialization paths available in Guildrun, matching your relics to your hero's evolved role is critical. A Tank hero specialized into "Damage Reduction" gains significantly more value from a "Universal Survival" relic than a "Healing Amplification" one.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify Your Core Carry">
    Determine which hero will deal the majority of your damage. If it's a Ranged hero, prioritize Attack Speed relics. If it's an Assassin, look for Burst or Finishing Power.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Class Synergy">
    Review your active specializations. If you have multiple heroes in the "Support" or "Healer" class, a Class-Scaling relic will provide 3x or 4x value compared to a single-target item.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Balance Defense and Utility">
    Ensure your frontline has enough survival relics to last at least 15 seconds. If they die too fast, your backline damage relics never get a chance to activate.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Pivot if Necessary">
    If the shop offers a legendary S-tier relic that doesn't fit your current team, consider using the "Respec" mechanic to change your heroes' specialization paths to match the new power source.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Hero Role | Recommended Relic Synergy | Priority Stat |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tank** | Damage Reduction, Global Health | **Defense** |
| **Healer** | Healing Amp, Ability Haste | **Sustain** |
| **Ranged** | Attack Speed, Multi-shot | **DPS** |
| **Assassin** | Crit Damage, Armor Pen | **Burst** |

<Callout type="info" title="The Respec Strategy">
Don't be afraid to respec. If you find a relic that grants massive bonuses to "Duelists" but you currently have "Assassins," the resource cost to switch specializations is often worth the massive power spike.
</Callout>

</Section>
