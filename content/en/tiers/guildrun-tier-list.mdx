<Section icon={Target} title="Class Specializations and Upgrade Priorities">

As you progress through a run, your heroes will gain access to various specialization paths. Guildrun features 75 specialization paths and 180 unique specializations, allowing you to transform basic recruits into highly specialized classes. Understanding when and how to upgrade these classes is just as important as drafting the correct heroes.

Upgrading a hero's rank unlocks new abilities and stat boosts that define their battlefield job. For example, a basic melee recruit can be developed into a durable Tank, a high-pressure Duelist, or a stealthy Assassin. Choosing the right specialization path ensures your team maintains a balanced distribution of damage, defense, and utility.

| Role | Battlefield Job | Specialization Focus | Multiclass Value |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|------------------|
| Tank | Hold frontline, absorb pressure | Durability, damage reduction, control | Pairs with healing or defensive support |
| Healer | Restore health, party stability | Healing strength, frequency, sustain | Pairs with defensive or utility paths |
| Ranged | Consistent backline damage | Attack speed, target coverage, scaling | Works with attack-speed bonuses |
| Duelist | Single-target pressure | Direct damage, repeated hits, survival | Benefits from defensive secondary paths |
| Assassin | Burst down priority targets | Burst damage, target access, finishing | Pairs with damage or speed paths |
| Hybrid | Fill multiple party needs | Balanced damage, support, or defense | Solves unexpected party weaknesses |

<Callout type="info" title="Specialization Rules">
Do not spread your upgrades too thinly across your entire roster. It is far more effective to fully specialize your main carry and primary tank before spending resources on minor upgrades for your reserve units.
</Callout>

If your current setup is struggling against specific enemy compositions, do not hesitate to utilize the respec mechanic. Respeccing allows you to reallocate a hero's specialization points, making it possible to transition a defensive hybrid into a high-damage carry if your team lacks the offensive power needed to clear late-game bosses.

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Team Compositions and Build Synergy">

Building a winning team in Guildrun is all about synergy. While individual hero strength is important, how those heroes interact with one another on the battlefield determines your ultimate success. The best compositions utilize shared effects, class bonuses, and strategic positioning to overwhelm the enemy.

One of the most effective strategies is the Single-Carry Scaling Build, which concentrates the majority of your offensive items and upgrades onto a single, highly protected damage dealer. This approach allows your carry to scale exponentially, while the rest of your team focuses entirely on keeping them alive.

| Composition Template | Core Frontline | Primary Carry | Support / Utility | Reserve Options |
|----------------------|----------------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Balanced First-Run | Durable Tank | Ranged Carry | Dedicated Healer | Flexible Hybrid |
| Lovers Shared-Effect | Protective Tank | Linked Carry | Buff Specialist | Alternative Carry |
| Single-Carry Scaling | Two Defensive Tanks | High-Scaling Carry | Sustain Support | Control Specialist |
| Double-Frontline | Primary & Secondary Tank | Ranged Carry | Utility Support | Reserve Tank |

<Callout type="warning" title="Synergy Trap">
Avoid the temptation to activate too many different shared-effect synergies at once. Forcing incompatible class combinations often results in a fragile team that lacks both sufficient defense and consistent damage output.
</Callout>

Positioning is another critical factor in team synergy. Always place your most durable frontline heroes in positions where they will intercept the initial wave of enemy pressure. Keep your fragile ranged carries and healers safely behind the tank, and use reserve units to swap in when facing specific counter-compositions.

</Section>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Your Core Trio">
    Begin your run by recruiting a balanced starting team consisting of one dedicated frontline tank, one reliable damage carry, and one support or utility unit. Avoid buying duplicate roles early, as this wastes valuable shards and limits your tactical options.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze Shop and Relic Offerings">
    Before spending your resources on upgrades, review the entire shop selection. Look for synergies between your current heroes and the available items or relics. Prioritize early economy relics that increase your gold or reroll efficiency, as these benefits compound over time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Commit to Specialization Paths">
    Once you have secured a stable core, begin investing in specialization paths that reinforce your heroes' primary roles. Focus on upgrading your main carry's damage scaling and your tank's survivability. Do not waste resources on minor upgrades for temporary recruits.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Positioning and Items">
    Before entering combat, arrange your heroes to maximize their effectiveness. Equip defensive items on your frontline to absorb pressure, and place offensive, attack-speed items on your protected carry. Keep your reserve units ready to adapt to difficult boss encounters.
  </Step>
</Steps>
