<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Beginner Strategy">

Succeeding in **Guildrun Steam** requires a shift from traditional RPG thinking to a "resource-first" mindset. You aren't just building a team; you are managing a shifting economy. The first few rifts determine the trajectory of your entire run. Following a structured approach to recruitment and itemization is essential for reaching the final boss.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Recruit a Balanced Foundation">
    Your starting gold should go toward a frontline hero (Tank), a reliable damage dealer (Ranged or Duelist), and a support unit. Avoid the temptation to buy three damage dealers; without a tank to absorb the opening rift pressure, your carries will fall before they can cycle their abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze the Shop Layout">
    Before clicking 'Reroll,' look at every item and hero offered. Sometimes a sub-optimal hero is worth buying if they come with a shared effect or a specialization that counters the current rift's enemy type. Rerolls should be saved for the mid-game when you are fishing for specific S-tier relics.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Commit to a Build Direction">
    By the third rift, you should have a clear idea of your "win condition." This might be a single-carry scaling build or a shared-effect composition like the **Lovers** synergy. Once you commit, only buy heroes and items that amplify this specific strategy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Specialization Ranks">
    As heroes gain ranks, they unlock specialization paths. Choose paths that solve your team's current weakness. If your frontline is melting, pick defensive specs. If you are timing out on boss fights, pivot your carry into a high-burst Assassin or sustained Ranged path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Optimize Positioning">
    Positioning is dynamic. In **Guildrun Steam**, enemies often target the closest unit or the unit with the highest health. Place your tank in the "hot zone" and tuck your supports in the back corners. Use reserve units to swap in when a specific enemy formation threatens your backline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Beginner Mistake">
Do not spread your items too thin. A single hero equipped with three synergistic items is significantly more powerful than three heroes each holding one random item. Concentrate your power on your primary carry.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Best Team Builds and Synergies">

Building a team in **Guildrun Steam** is about more than just picking strong heroes; it's about creating a machine where the parts amplify each other. Below are the most successful archetypes discovered by the community during the 2026 demo phase.

### The Balanced First-Run Build
This is the safest way to complete your first full run. It relies on a "Holy Trinity" structure that handles most enemy formations with minimal micro-management.
- **Frontline**: A high-health Tank with damage reduction specializations.
- **Carry**: A Ranged hero focused on attack speed.
- **Support**: A Healer with party-wide buffs.
- **Reserve**: A Duelist to swap in for boss fights.

### The Lovers Shared-Effect Build
This high-risk, high-reward build focuses on the unique "Lovers" mechanic where two heroes share stats and effects.
- **Core**: Two heroes with the Lovers tag.
- **Strategy**: Protect the pair at all costs. Give the most vulnerable member defensive items while the other member carries offensive relics.
- **Weakness**: If one member of the pair dies early, the other loses significant bonuses.

| Build Name | Core Strength | Item Priority | Difficulty Rating |

| **Balanced** | High stability, low risk | Defense on Tank, DPS on Carry | 1/5 (Beginner) |

| **Single-Carry** | Concentrated power | All offense on one Hero | 4/5 (Advanced) |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Items and Relics Tier List">

Items and Relics are the "modifiers" that turn a standard team into an unstoppable force. With over 300 relics planned, the current meta focuses on universal effects that provide value regardless of your specific hero choices.

### S-Tier Equipment (Must-Pick)
1. **Class-Scaling Relics**: These improve every hero of a specific class (e.g., all Tanks gain 20% HP). These are the most powerful late-game items.
2. **Attack-Speed Effects**: In an autobattler, more attacks mean more ability procs. These are universally good on any damage dealer.
3. **Early Economy Relics**: Items that give extra gold or free rerolls. Picking these in Rift 1 or 2 can double your total power by the end of the run.
4. **Universal Survival**: Flat damage reduction or shields that trigger at the start of combat. Essential for higher difficulty tiers.

### Item Compatibility Table
| Category | Best Hero Match | Power Level | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lifesteal** | Duelists / Assassins | High | Keeps front-line carries alive |
| **Mana Regen** | Healers / Mages | Medium | Faster ability cycles |
| **Armor Pen** | Ranged / Duelists | High | Essential for late-game bosses |
| **Max HP** | Tanks | Very High | Direct scaling with survival |

<Callout type="tip" title="Inventory Management">
You can move items between heroes in the shop phase. If you are about to face a boss with heavy backline damage, move your defensive items from your tank to your carry temporarily to ensure they survive the initial burst.
</Callout>

### Endless Mode Strategy
In Endless Mode, the enemies scale infinitely. Standard damage numbers will eventually become irrelevant. To climb the leaderboards, you must focus on **percentage-based damage**, **infinite scaling relics**, and **crowd control**. A team that can stun or freeze enemies is often better than a team that deals raw damage in the deep stages of Endless Mode.

<Callout type="warning" title="Endless Mode Warning">
