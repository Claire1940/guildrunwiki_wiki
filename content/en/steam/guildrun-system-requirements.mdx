<Section icon={Cpu} title="Guildrun System Requirements: PC Specifications">

Before diving into the strategic depth of this single-player PvE roguelike autobattler, verifying that your PC hardware aligns with the official **guildrun system requirements** is the first step toward a seamless gaming experience. Developed by Leyline, the game features complex combat calculations, status tracking, and vibrant visual effects that require stable hardware to prevent late-game slowdowns. 

Because autobattlers process hundreds of calculations simultaneously—such as active relic effects, stat scaling, and individual hero specializations—having a capable processor is just as important as your graphics card. Below are the minimum and recommended hardware specifications to run the game efficiently.

### Minimum System Requirements

The minimum specifications are designed to target 1080p resolution at 30 to 45 frames per second during standard combat encounters. This configuration is ideal for budget-friendly setups, older desktop PCs, and work laptops utilizing integrated graphics.

| Component | Minimum Specification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OS** | Windows 10 64-bit |
| **Processor** | Intel Core i3-6100 / AMD FX-4350 |
| **Memory** | 4 GB RAM |
| **Graphics** | NVIDIA GeForce GTX 660 / AMD Radeon HD 7850 |
| **DirectX** | Version 11 |
| **Storage** | 2 GB available space |

### Recommended System Requirements

To enjoy the game at a locked 60 frames per second with high-quality particle effects, fast loading times, and zero stuttering during intense late-game Endless Mode runs, aiming for the recommended specifications is highly advised.

| Component | Recommended Specification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **OS** | Windows 10/11 64-bit |
| **Processor** | Intel Core i5-4460 / AMD Ryzen 3 1200 |
| **Memory** | 8 GB RAM |
| **Graphics** | NVIDIA GeForce GTX 960 / AMD Radeon RX 470 |
| **DirectX** | Version 11 |
| **Storage** | 4 GB available space |

<Callout type="warning" title="64-Bit OS Required">

<Section icon={Wrench} title="Graphics Optimization & Performance Tips">

If your PC sits closer to the minimum hardware specifications, you may experience minor frame drops when multiple high-tier specializations trigger their visual effects simultaneously. Fortunately, the in-game settings menu offers several toggles to recover lost performance without sacrificing visual clarity.

By systematically adjusting resource-heavy settings, you can maintain a stable frame rate even during chaotic late-game rounds where screen space is crowded with summons, status indicators, and area-of-effect spells.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Configure Display Mode">
    Switch from Exclusive Fullscreen to Windowed Borderless. This reduces input latency and prevents stuttering if you frequently alt-tab to check hero tier lists or build guides.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Lower Shadow Resolution">
    Shadows represent the single largest GPU tax in the game. Lowering shadow quality from High to Medium or Low can boost your average frame rate by up to 15% on older graphics cards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Limit Maximum Frame Rate">
    Match your frame rate cap directly to your monitor's refresh rate. If you are playing on a standard 60Hz screen, capping the game at 60 FPS prevents unnecessary GPU strain and thermal throttling.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Disable Screen Shake">
    Turn off the screen shake toggle in the accessibility menu. This not only improves performance during rapid attack sequences but also makes it much easier to track unit health bars during combat.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Integrated Graphics Performance">
Following these optimization steps allows modern integrated graphics chips, such as AMD Radeon 680M or Intel Iris Xe, to easily maintain a locked 60 FPS at 1080p resolution.
</Callout>

</Section>
