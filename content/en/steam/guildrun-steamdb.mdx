<Section icon={Map} title="Complete Walkthrough for Your First Successful Run">

Navigating your first run in Guildrun requires a clear understanding of the game's economy, recruitment systems, and combat preparation. Since resources are scarce, especially in the early stages, wasting Shards on unnecessary shop refreshes can severely cripple your progression. Instead, focus on building a stable foundation that can adapt to whatever items or relics the game offers.

The progression loop is divided into distinct phases: recruitment, shop review, specialization selection, and positioning. By mastering each phase, you can consistently clear the initial difficulties and prepare your roster for the brutal modifiers found in higher-tier rifts.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure a Balanced Frontline First">
    Begin your run by recruiting at least one durable frontline hero. Your primary tank is responsible for absorbing the opening enemy pressure, giving your backline damage dealers and support units enough time to activate their abilities. Avoid buying multiple fragile carries early, as they will quickly perish without adequate protection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Inspect the Shop Before Spending">
    Always review the entire shop selection before committing your Shards. Compare new hero recruits, duplicate upgrades, items, and potential specialization paths. Buying immediate power upgrades is generally safer than holding onto resources for a speculative build that may never materialize.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select Synergistic Specializations">
    As your heroes level up, choose specialization paths that reinforce their primary battlefield jobs. For example, upgrade your tank's defensive capabilities or focus your carry's path toward consistent physical or magical damage. Do not mix unrelated stats on a single hero.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Equip and Position Your Team">
    Place defensive items on your frontline units and offensive scaling items on your main damage carry. Position your tank in the most exposed hexes to intercept enemy movement, while keeping your fragile supports and ranged damage dealers shielded in the back row.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
Rerolling the shop too early is a common beginner mistake. Save your Shards for upgrading existing heroes and securing high-tier items that guarantee immediate power spikes.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Top Team Compositions and Build Strategies">

Constructing a winning team in Guildrun is an art of balancing damage, defense, and utility. The most successful builds rely on shared effects, class synergies, and relic-driven scaling. When building your team, always keep a flexible reserve unit in mind to pivot your strategy if the shop stops offering upgrades for your primary carry.

Let us examine the four most dominant team builds in the current meta. Each build utilizes unique positioning and itemization rules to maximize survival and damage output.

| Build Name | Core Roles | Positioning Rule | Primary Relic Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Balanced First-Run** | Tank, Carry, Support, Reserve | Tank exposed, Carry protected | General resource or stat bonuses |
| **Lovers Shared-Effect** | Linked Lovers pair, Tank, Support | Keep Lovers pair safe from AOE | Shared-effect amplifiers |
| **Single-Carry Scaling** | Main Carry, Two Tanks, Utility | Maximize spacing to protect Carry | Carry damage and attack speed |
| **Double-Frontline Control** | Primary Tank, Off-Tank, Backline Carry | Split pressure between frontliners | Relics rewarding extended combat |

The **Balanced First-Run** build is highly recommended for players still learning the enemy attack patterns. It relies on a simple yet effective formula: one incredibly tanky unit absorbing all single-target threat, one ranged carry providing consistent damage from safety, and a dedicated healer keeping the frontline healthy. The fourth slot is reserved for a utility hero who can stun enemies or apply armor shred.

For advanced players, the **Lovers Shared-Effect** build offers unparalleled damage scaling but requires precise positioning. If both linked units are hit by the same area-of-effect (AOE) spell, your run can end instantly. Therefore, separating them on the grid while maintaining their link is essential for success.
