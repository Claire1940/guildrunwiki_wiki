<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Completing Your First Run">

Navigating your first successful run in the **guildrun demo** requires a disciplined approach to resource management and team composition. The following steps outline a reliable strategy for beginners to reach the final boss of the demo's initial difficulty.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Recruit a Balanced Core">
    Start your run by securing three basic roles: a durable frontline tank, a reliable damage dealer, and a support unit. Avoid the temptation to buy every hero; focus on units that complement each other's keywords. A protected carry is always more effective than three unprotected ones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze the Shop Before Buying">
    Before spending your Shards, look at everything the shop offers. Compare new recruits against potential upgrades for your current roster. Early in the run, prioritize immediate power over speculative late-game builds to ensure you survive the first few rifts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Commit to Specializations">
    As your heroes rank up, you will choose between different specialization paths. Select upgrades that reinforce the hero's current job. For example, if your frontline is struggling, choose defensive specializations over minor damage boosts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Optimize Item Placement">
    Items and relics should be distributed based on hero roles. Defensive equipment belongs on heroes absorbing the initial enemy pressure, while offensive scaling items should go to heroes likely to survive until the end of the round.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Positioning and Reserve Management">
    Arrange your durable heroes to intercept pressure. Use the reserve slots for situational specialists or future upgrades. Swapping a hero from the reserve can often be the difference between a loss and a victory against specific boss mechanics.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Resource Management">
Do not exhaust your Shards on rerolls during the first three rounds. It is better to have a slightly suboptimal team with a strong economy than a perfect team that cannot afford rank upgrades.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Hero Classes and Specialization Roles">

In the **guildrun demo**, heroes are defined by their class roles and the specialization paths they take. Understanding these roles is essential for building a team that can handle the diverse threats found in the rifts.

| Class Role | Battlefield Job | Primary Focus | Multiclass Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tank** | Absorb pressure | Durability/Control | Pairs with Healer |
| **Healer** | Party sustain | Healing/Protection | Pairs with Support |
| **Ranged** | Consistent DPS | Attack Speed/Scaling | Works with Assassins |
| **Duelist** | 1v1 Pressure | Burst/Sustain | High Solo Potential |
| **Assassin** | Target priority | Burst Damage | High Risk/Reward |
| **Hybrid** | Flex role | Balanced Stats | Fills party gaps |

Specializations allow a hero to pivot their role mid-run. A Tank might specialize into a "Guardian" for pure defense or a "Brawler" to add crowd control. The flexibility of the 180 total specializations means that no two runs with the same hero roster will feel identical.

<Callout type="success" title="Synergy Bonus">
Matching specializations across your team (e.g., multiple "Frost" or "Bleed" paths) often yields hidden power spikes that are stronger than the sum of their individual parts.
</Callout>
